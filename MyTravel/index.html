About

Being a middle-aged person has many quirks. Especially when you realized that you no longer have the stamina of the younger you. That doesn't mean you can't explore the world. It just means you have to do it differently.

Join me as I share my travel experiences, tips, and stories from being agile to being slow.

Places I've been

Over the years, I've had the privilege to visit several amazing places. That is, before Covid hits.

Newtown A charming town with a rich history and vibrant culture. If you love old township, you have to come here!
Parramatta CBD A bustling suburb that can compete with Sydney CBD. They don't call it CBD for nothing!
Blue Mountains A breathtaking natural wonder with stunning vistas, hiking trails, and charming towns. A must-visit for nature lovers!
Hunter Valley A renowned wine region known for its picturesque vineyards, gourmet food, and stunning landscapes. A must-visit for food and wine lovers!

All these places were amazing, until I was down with Covid. When I finally recovered, I noticed that I could no longer have the stamina to walk long distances. When you being out of breath becomes your main focus instead of the breathtaking scenery, it's time to rethink your travel plans.

Travel Tips

Being a aged traveller with decreased stamina, I've learned a few tips to make travel more enjoyable:

Plan Small Don't over-schedule your trip. Know your limits and allow for plenty of downtime.
Choose Accessibility Opt for places that offers accessibility features, depending on what your body needs most.
Stay Hydrated Carry a water bottle and drink plenty of fluids to stay hydrated, especially in hot weather.
Pack Light Bring only essentials to avoid carrying heavy luggage. Use a backpack or a rolling suitcase for easier mobility. You don't want your luggage to be a burden.
Take Your Time Similar to Plan Small, don't rush through your trip. Pace yourself and allow plenty of time to move around.
Listen to Your Body, but just not over worrying Pay attention to how you're feeling and take breaks as needed. But don't start panicking if something does not feel right. Just slow down, or stop and reassess.
